# Getting Started

1) Click on the "Proposal" tab along the left, and give your plan a name!  You can change it later.

2) Do you already know your "Target"?  Click to that tab, and then enter it in the panel to the right.

3) Go ahead and make an "Observation" by either clicking that tab or the green  "Obs+" for each target.  For now, you only need to know the wavelength to observe and what S/N is required.  But you can choose an instrument and set an exposure time, instead. The "Group+" button allows logic: you can repeat that observation.  Or, do something else, and select a different observation to follow.

4) Adjust "Constraints" and "Scheduling" (lower tabs to the left).  Your goal: relax those as much as possible, to make observations more likely to be scheduled!  If you already know when that can be, now's the time to enter all of those windows.

5) Play around, and try a different way!  "Undo/Redo" buttons are along the top-left, next to "Obs+".

6) Complete instructions are available in the "User Manual" in the drop-down menu at top-right! 

7) Come back later.  Your program has already been saved to your ORCID ID, if you didn't visit as a "Guest."
